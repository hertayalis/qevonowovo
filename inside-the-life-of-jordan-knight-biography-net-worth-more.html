Jordan Knight is an American singer, songwriter, and actor, best known for being a member of the popular boy band, New Kids on the Block (NKOTB). Here is a brief overview of his life, career, and net worth.

Biography:

Jordan Nathaniel Marcel Knight was born on May 17, 1970, in Worcester, Massachusetts. He was the fifth of six children born to Marlene and Allan Knight. He grew up in the town of Auburn, Massachusetts, where he attended Auburn High School.

Career:

Knight’s music career began in 1984 when he joined the boy band, New Kids on the Block, alongside Donnie Wahlberg, Joey McIntyre, Danny Wood, and Jonathan Knight. The group became one of the biggest pop acts of the late 1980s and early 1990s, selling over 80 million records worldwide.

After the band split in 1994, Knight pursued a solo career, releasing his self-titled debut album in 1999. The album included the hit singles “Give It to You” and “I Could Never Take the Place of Your Man.” Knight continued to release solo albums throughout the 2000s, including “Jordan Knight Performs New Kids on the Block: The Remix Album” and “Unfinished.”

In 2008, NKOTB reunited and went on a highly successful tour. The group has since released several new albums and continues to tour.

Aside from music, Knight has also acted in various TV shows and films, including “Love on the Line,” “The Surreal Life,” and “The Real O’Neals.”

Net Worth:

Jordan Knight’s net worth is estimated to be around $18 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. The majority of his wealth comes from his music career, including his solo work and his time with NKOTB. He has also earned money from acting and endorsements.
