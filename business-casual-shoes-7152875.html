8 Business Casual Shoes That Go With Everything

August 2024 · 3 minute read

It is a fact universally acknowledged that a business casual wardrobe doesn’t have to be a boring one. That said, choosing the right pair of business casual shoes can be a bit of a challenge. There’s a fine line to toe, if you catch our drift, between expressing your personal style and still keeping things office-appropriate. It can be easy to stick to the same tried, true, and timeless black pointed-toe pumps for all your business casual shoe needs, but that can also get boring. If you find yourself sick of the same old slingbacks or flats, these eight business casual shoe ideas just might put a pep in your step.

Rather than reinvent the wheel, the business casual shoes below mix things up without straying from what works. You’ll see familiar, foot-friendly styles amped up with details that’ll make you want to wear them seven days a week and on holidays. 

Printed Pumps 

Low heels might appear unassuming next to sky-high stilettos or stacked platforms, but a bold print changes everything. A pump with a low heel and an eye-catching pattern make a fashion statement without hurting your feet and can tie your entire wardrobe together. 

Quilted Flats

Flats are a familiar favorite in the business casual fashion world because, let’s face it, they’re easy to wear and they’re comfortable as heck. A simple detail like the quilting on these two-toned flats makes them the perfect choice for slacks on Monday and your fave baggy boyfriend jeans on the weekend. 

Bold Block Heels

Block heels can be hit or miss, but there’s no denying that they make sense with almost every business casual outfit you can dream up. When it comes to keeping things fun and fresh, opting for an eye-catching color or pattern really levels up these classic heels. 

Pointed-Toe Loafers

Lately, loafers have experienced a bit of a punk revival, which we love, no shade. But there’s another very business casual friendly way to wear this style of shoe. A pair of loafers with a pointed toe serves up more feminine energy while remaining trendy. 

Ballet Flats

You could say that ribbons are the current disruptors of the ballerina flat world. Ribbons don’t just add delicacy to a pair of flats, they’re also evocative of the style’s namesake which is a major spring trend. Just ask Bella Hadid.

Velvet Slippers

Fun and flirty, velvet slippers are an on-the-go go-to. Finding one with a subtle print in a contrasting finish — like the gold hearts on these mauve velvet flats — will breathe new life into this classic style. 

Moto-Loafers

Remember those punk rock loafers we mentioned earlier? They also get the business casual seal of approval. A thick platform, subtle silver or gold hardware, and a rounded toe keep this style from leaning too far into casual.

Metallic Slingbacks 

Slingbacks aren’t likely to go anywhere anytime soon. There are simply too many fun ways to wear this style of shoe. You can pair them with thick tights or socks or slip in your bare feet without budging from the sweet spot between bossed up and being the boss. To tip the scales even further into the fashion-forward direction, try working a pair of metallic slingbacks into your business casual shoe rotation. After all, metallics are basically neutrals, right?
