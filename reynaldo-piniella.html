Reynaldo Piniella (born on October 29, 1990; Age:31) is an American actor from NYC who has been gaining attention for making his appearance as Julio in the second season of “Reservation Dogs Season 2,” which aired on Hulu on August 3.

Piniella has been associated with the acting industry for a long time and has played chiefly minor roles or worked as a voice artist, but apart from that, He is an acting teacher at AMDA College of the Performing Arts and has done extensive work as a theatre artist.

This American youngster is not only an award-winning performer but also a fellow of Theatre Communications Group. He was mentioned in American Theatre Magazine a few years ago as a theatre worker. According to his IMDB profile, he is currently working on two upcoming movies, “Spell” and “The Driven.”

Reynaldo Grew Up in a Violent Neighborhood

On October 29, 1990, Reynaldo Piniella (age 31) took birth in Eastern New York, USA. He was a toddler when his father and mother separated after divorce, so he got raised by a single mother. He used to live in one of the most violent places in America.

The kids of his neighborhood were involved in different forms of crimes involving theft, robbery, and felony. On the other hand, Piniella spent most of his time playing video games or reading books.

John Browne High, the school Reynaldo attended, was also considered one of the poorest institutes (in NYC) in terms of education. There used to be armed guards outside the school every time. Luckily, at John Browne High, Piniella met an English teacher with an art background. He introduced the young guy to the theatre.

Reynaldo instantly fell in love with the arts. The shy guy who used to stay all the time in his room started performing on stage. Upon completing high school, he joined Brooklyn College to pursue a bachelor’s in theatre history, but in 2012 dropped out of college for his first show.

From Broadway to Hollywood

Reynaldo’s acting journey as an artist started when a teacher at his school introduced him to the art of stage performance. As time passed, he became more and more engaged in theatre.

He even volunteered to teach acting for free to those in need. In one of his interviews, he accepted he was never prepared for Broadway and had not imagined working in such a prestigious institution.

In 2011, he landed his first role in a TV show. It was an Erich Sturm-directed crime drama titled “Dr. G: America’s Most Shocking Cases.” The following year he worked on three television programs, namely “Celebrity Ghost Stories,” “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit,” and “Fatal Encounters.”

From 2010 to 2014, he appeared in a total of 11 shows, including his major break, “Token: The Web Series,” where he got to play a recurring role.

On the one hand, Reynaldo was getting work in new shows one after the other; on the other hand, he also entered the film industry with “The Class Reunion.” When it came to movies, he majorly worked in short films, including “10 Seconds Barrier,” for which he received the best actor award.

Some of his major projects include “Waves,” “Madeline’s Madeline,” “One Percent More Humid,” “When I’m with You,” and “Reservation Dogs.”
